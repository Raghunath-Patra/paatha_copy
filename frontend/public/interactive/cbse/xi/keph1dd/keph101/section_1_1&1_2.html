🔬 SI Base Units

📏 The International System of Units (SI)

Système Internationale d' Unites - established by BIPM in 1971

Seven fundamental base units form the foundation of all measurements

SI System: The internationally accepted system of units, replacing older systems like CGS, FPS, and MKS. It uses a decimal system making conversions simple and convenient.

The Seven SI Base Units

📏
Length
metre (m)
Defined by the speed of light in vacuum: c = 299,792,458 m/s
⚖️
Mass
kilogram (kg)
Defined by Planck's constant: h = 6.62607015 × 10⁻³⁴ J·s
⏰
Time
second (s)
Based on caesium-133 frequency: Δνcs = 9,192,631,770 Hz
⚡
Electric Current
ampere (A)
Defined by elementary charge: e = 1.602176634 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
🌡️
Temperature
kelvin (K)
Based on Boltzmann constant: k = 1.380649 × 10⁻²³ J/K
🧪
Amount of Substance
mole (mol)
Contains exactly 6.02214076 × 10²³ elementary entities (Avogadro's number)
💡
Luminous Intensity
candela (cd)
Luminous efficacy of 540 × 10¹² Hz radiation: Kcd = 683 lm/W
🔑 Key Points:
• All other units are derived from these seven base units
• Definitions are based on fundamental physical constants
• Ensures universal consistency and precision in measurements

📐 Angles & Dimensional Measurements

🔄 Supplementary Units

Two additional dimensionless units for angular measurements

Essential for describing rotational and solid geometry

Interactive Angle Demonstrations

📐 Plane Angle (Radian)

dθ = ds/r

Arc length ÷ Radius

1 radian ≈ 57.3°

Full circle = 2π radians

🌐 Solid Angle (Steradian)

dΩ = dA/r²

Surface area ÷ Radius²

Full sphere = 4π steradians

Dimensionless quantity

📐
Plane Angle
radian (rad)
The angle subtended when arc length equals radius. Dimensionless quantity.
🌐
Solid Angle
steradian (sr)
The solid angle subtended when surface area equals radius squared. Dimensionless quantity.

📊 SI Prefixes Reference

📈
Large Scale Prefixes
T (tera) = 10¹²
G (giga) = 10⁹
M (mega) = 10⁶
k (kilo) = 10³
📉
Small Scale Prefixes
m (milli) = 10⁻³
μ (micro) = 10⁻⁶
n (nano) = 10⁻⁹
p (pico) = 10⁻¹²
🔢
Common Examples
1 km = 1000 m
1 mm = 0.001 m
1 MHz = 10⁶ Hz
1 μs = 10⁻⁶ s
