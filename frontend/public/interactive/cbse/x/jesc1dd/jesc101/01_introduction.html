Laboratory Experiments - Virtual Simulations

🔥 Activity 1.1: Burning Magnesium Ribbon
🧪 Magnesium Ribbon Burning Experiment
Mg ribbon → Heat + Light → White MgO powder
⚡ Energy: Released
🌡️ Heat: High
💡 Light: Bright

📋 Observations:

  • Magnesium ribbon burns with a dazzling white flame
  • A white powdery substance (ash) is formed
  • The white powder is magnesium oxide (MgO)
  • Chemical equation: 2Mg(s) + O₂(g) → 2MgO(s)
⚗️ Activity 1.2: Lead Nitrate + Potassium Iodide
💧 Lead Nitrate Solution (Clear)
💧 Potassium Iodide Solution (Clear)
Pb(NO₃)₂ + 2KI → PbI₂ (yellow precipitate) + 2KNO₃
🧪 Double Displacement Reaction

📋 Observations:

  • A yellow precipitate is formed
  • The precipitate is lead iodide (PbI₂)
  • Formation of precipitate indicates chemical reaction
  • Chemical equation: Pb(NO₃)₂(aq) + 2KI(aq) → PbI₂(s) + 2KNO₃(aq)
💨 Activity 1.3: Zinc + Hydrochloric Acid
🔩 Zinc Granules
🧪 + Dilute HCl
Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂ (gas bubbles)
💨 Gas Evolution Reaction
💨 Gas: H₂ (Hydrogen)
🌡️ Heat: Warm
⚡ Energy: Released

📋 Observations:

  • Gas bubbles evolve from zinc surface
  • The test tube feels warm (exothermic reaction)
  • Hydrogen gas is evolved
  • Chemical equation: Zn(s) + 2HCl(aq) → ZnCl₂(aq) + H₂(g)

Signs of Chemical Reactions

📝 Which of the following observations indicate that a chemical reaction has taken place?
🌈 Change in color
💨 Evolution of gas
🌡️ Change in temperature
⚡ Change in state
📏 Change in size only
💎 Formation of precipitate
💡 Remember: Chemical reactions involve changes at the molecular level. The signs we observe are external manifestations of these internal changes. Not all physical changes are chemical reactions!
