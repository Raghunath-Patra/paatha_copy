🌱 Pre-fertilisation: The Foundation of Plant Reproduction

Before fertilisation can occur, flowering plants must undergo complex developmental and cellular processes that prepare the male and female reproductive structures for successful gamete fusion.

🔬 Key Processes

  • Microsporogenesis (male gamete formation)
  • Megasporogenesis (female gamete formation)
  • Pollen grain maturation
  • Embryo sac development
  • Pollination mechanisms

🎯 Learning Objectives

  • Explore cellular processes in gamete formation

🦋 Advanced Pollination Mechanisms and Strategies

🌪️ Abiotic Pollination

Wind Pollination (Anemophily):
  • Light, dry pollen grains
  • Large quantities produced
  • Feathery stigmas for pollen capture
  • Examples: Grasses, corn, wheat
Water Pollination (Hydrophily):
  • Ribbon-like pollen grains
  • Mucilaginous coating protection
  • Surface or underwater transfer
  • Examples: Vallisneria, seagrasses

🐝 Biotic Pollination

Insect Pollination (Entomophily):
  • Bright colors and patterns
  • Sweet fragrances and nectar
  • Sticky pollen grains
  • Landing platforms (petals)
Other Pollinators:
  • Birds (Ornithophily) - bright red/orange
  • Bats (Chiropterophily) - night blooming
  • Mammals - large, robust flowers

🧬 Molecular Pollen-Pistil Interactions

🤝 Recognition and Compatibility

The pollen-pistil interaction involves complex molecular dialogue between pollen grain components and pistil surface proteins that determine compatibility and guide fertilization.

🔍 Recognition Molecules
  • S-proteins: Self-incompatibility recognition
  • Stigma proteins: Species-specific binding
  • Pollen coat proteins: Identity markers
  • Calcium channels: Signal transduction
⚡ Signaling Pathways
  • Compatible: Pollen tube growth promoted
  • Incompatible: Growth arrested or tube burst
  • Ca²⁺ oscillations: Growth regulation
  • ROS signaling: Stress responses
