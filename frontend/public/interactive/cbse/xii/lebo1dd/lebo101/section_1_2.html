üå± Pre-fertilisation Events

Before fertilisation can occur, flowering plants must develop specialized reproductive structures and undergo specific processes:

  • Androecium Development: Male reproductive organs (stamens) produce pollen grains
  • Gynoecium Development: Female reproductive organs (pistils) develop ovules
  • Microsporogenesis: Formation of male gametes through meiosis
  • Megasporogenesis: Formation of female gametes through meiosis
  • Pollination: Transfer of pollen from anther to stigma
  • Pollen-Pistil Interaction: Recognition and compatibility processes

üè∫ Stamen Structure and Function

The stamen consists of two main parts working together for pollen production:

üåø Structural Components

  • Filament: Long, slender stalk supporting the anther
  • Anther: Bilobed structure containing microsporangia
  • Microsporangia: Four pollen sacs (two per lobe)
  • Tapetum: Innermost layer nourishing pollen development

üîÑ Functional Processes

  • Microsporogenesis: Meiotic division forming microspores
  • Pollen maturation: Development of male gametophytes
  • Dehiscence: Opening of anthers to release pollen
  • Pollen dispersal: Transfer via wind, water, or animals

üå∫ Pistil Structure and Ovule Development

The pistil forms the female reproductive system with three distinct parts:

üéØ Pistil Components

  • Stigma: Receptive surface for pollen
  • Style: Connecting tube between stigma and ovary
  • Ovary: Contains ovules and develops into fruit
  • Placenta: Tissue bearing ovules

ü•ö Ovule Structure

  • Integuments: Protective layers around nucellus
  • Micropyle: Opening for pollen tube entry
  • Nucellus: Central tissue containing embryo sac
  • Embryo Sac: 7-celled female gametophyte

ü¶ã Pollination Mechanisms

Transfer of pollen from anther to stigma through various agents:

üå™Ô∏è Abiotic Pollination

  • Wind: Light, non-sticky pollen
  • Water: Ribbon-like pollen grains

üêù Biotic Pollination

  • Insects: Bees, butterflies, beetles
  • Birds: Hummingbirds, sunbirds
  • Mammals: Bats, primates

üéØ Pollination Types

  • Autogamy: Self-pollination
  • Geitonogamy: Same plant
  • Xenogamy: Different plants

ü§ù Pollen-Pistil Interaction

Complex molecular dialogue between pollen and pistil determining compatibility:

  1. Pollen Recognition: Chemical identification of pollen type
  2. Compatibility Assessment: Accept compatible, reject incompatible pollen
  3. Pollen Germination: Formation of pollen tube through germ pore
  4. Tube Growth: Navigation through stigma and style tissues
  5. Ovule Entry: Guidance by filiform apparatus of synergids
  6. Gamete Delivery: Release of male gametes for fertilization
