Historical Discovery

🎯 Historical Discovery: Around 400 BC, the Pythagoreans in Greece discovered numbers that could not be written as fractions. This revolutionary discovery challenged their entire mathematical worldview and led to one of the greatest advances in mathematical thinking!

📋 Definition

A number 's' is called irrational if it cannot be written in the form p/q, where p and q are integers and q ≠ 0.

Examples: √2, √3, √15, π, e, 0.101001000100001...

Geometric Construction of √2

🏗️ How the Greeks Discovered √2

Watch the step-by-step construction that shows √2 exists and can be located on the number line!

Construction Steps:

  1. Draw a unit square OABC (each side = 1 unit)
  2. Draw the diagonal OB using Pythagoras theorem: OB = √(1² + 1²) = √2
  3. Use a compass with center O and radius OB
  4. Draw an arc that intersects the number line at point P
  5. Point P represents √2 on the number line!
