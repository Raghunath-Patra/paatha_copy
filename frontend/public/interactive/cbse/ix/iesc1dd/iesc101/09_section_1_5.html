ð Definition of Evaporation

Evaporation is the phenomenon of change of liquid into vapors at any temperature below its boiling point.

It happens because particles at the surface with higher kinetic energy break away from intermolecular forces and escape as vapor.

ð§ Cooling Mechanism

When liquid evaporates, high-energy particles escape, leaving behind particles with lower average kinetic energy. This results in cooling of the remaining liquid and surrounding surfaces.
